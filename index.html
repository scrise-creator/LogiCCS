About the project

Maritime logistics for future CCS value chains

LogiCCS develops sustainable and energy-efficient maritime logistics solutions for transporting captured carbon dioxide (CO₂) in the Nordic and Baltic region.

The project focuses on improving the efficiency of CO₂ transport, identifying suitable geological storage options, and supporting the development of cost-effective maritime infrastructure for Carbon Capture and Storage.

Objectives

What the project will address

Efficient CCS logistics

Develop cost- and energy-efficient maritime logistics solutions for transporting captured CO₂ and identify optimal storage alternatives across the region.

Future ship design

Develop recommendations for LCO₂ carrier design, fleet composition, routing strategies and low-emission maritime transport technologies.

Sustainability assessment

Evaluate environmental, economic and social impacts across the CCS value chain to support sustainable deployment.

Policy and regulation

Identify regulatory barriers and opportunities and provide recommendations to facilitate cross-border CCS infrastructure and market development.

Consortium

Project partners

The LogiCCS consortium brings together partners from Sweden, Norway, Denmark and Latvia, combining expertise in CCS, maritime logistics, ship design, sustainability, policy, technology development and industry.
